DEKITI-TIRSIA-SIRADAS

DEKITI-TIRSIA-SIRADAS - is a unique system of all Filipino art. It is identified with Kali in its philosophy and cultural forms with identity in its genetical values manifested in the physical and intellectual faculties as observed and found in the ability and skill of DEKITI-TIRSIA-SIRADAS TORTAL SYSTEM.

Dekiti-Tirsia-Siradas Tortal System

  • was a formation of intellectual expertise of the Tortal’s brothers namely: Conrado, Balbino, Francisco, Teodorico, along with their father Segundito and great grandfather Norberto. Those were the pillars of the DEKITI-TIRSIA-SIRADAS TORTAL SYSTEM family. System now survived by Grand Master Jerson (Nene) P. Tortal and his son Jerson E. Tortal Jr.
  • was popularized in different States of America by Grandmaster Jerson (Nene) Tortal from the province of Talisay, Negros Occidental, Philippines and expanded to North and South America including Western Europe and Asia.
  • made a legendary history in the martial arts world. The Dekiti-Tirsia-Siradas System known as the safety baton and surviving edge weapon was the most accepted system as a liability factor saver for Law Enforcement, Military, Security and Executives.
  • in terms of sports program, (tournaments, demonstration, etc) a series of demonstrations, tournaments were held in different parts of the Philippines, in schools, universities and private companies.
  • is a medieval art of the modern times and the living past. It has been proven and continued to be effective and devastating. It is a complete system from philosophical, cultural and fighting values. Only Grandmaster Jerson (Nene) P. Tortal and his only son Jerson E. Tortal Jr. can explain the real meaning of DEKITI-TIRSIA-SIRADAS TORTAL SSYTEM in its fighting spirit and fighting forms.

  • Address your instructor(s) and your fellow members with their proper title: Sir, Ma’am, Maestro, Guro, Kuya, Ate, etc.
  • Direction by instructors must be obeyed first without question; questions will be accommodated later.
  • If the given activity proves to be difficult, continue it to the best of your ability. AVOID negative criticism of any one..
  • Wear a complete proper uniform during practice, seminar, competition and exhibition/demonstration.
  • AVOID removal of any part of the uniform during the training session unless given permission to.
  • Your uniform must be clean and washed. Your belt must not be washed, but air dry. Your belt symbolically “contains the spirit” of your hard training.
  • REMOVE all jewelry and wrist ornaments before training (earring’s, necklaces, rings, watches, etc.)
  • Fingernails and toenails MUST be trimmed short and be clean. Long hair MUST be tied back.
  • PAY YOUR FEES ON TIME. It is considered poor form to have the instructor ask for payment.

Everyone, regardless of age, grade or status, must adhere to these rules. These are enacted in order to facilitate the smooth, safe running and preparation of all training halls and to assure that all members know each other’s rank, duties, and responsibilities.

It is a privilege to be an IMAFP member. With this membership come certain responsibilities. Our Bothoan, instructors, our style Modern Arnis is something to be proud of. Our art is highly regarded and well respected in the martial arts world and the general community. It is our duty to avoid anything that may embarrass, shame or dishonor our Filipino heritage, culture and values found in Arnis de Mano.

It is natural to be proud of Arnis de Mano and of IMAFP. It is inevitable that you will talk to your family and friends about the Filipino Martial arts.

  • Resist the urge to brag or to show off your skills. The techniques you have learned have the potential to harm others.
  • Be careful not to injure family, friends, yourself, and others, especially if somebody requests you to demonstrate a technique to inspire motivation in joining the association.
  • Avoid display of your uniform in the street or any public place, except on necessary occasions or as permitted by your instructor.
  • When witnessing other styles and\or other martial arts, avoid criticism. Look instead for the positive aspects of that particular style. All martial arts have something of value that one can learn from.
  • When all attempts have failed to use peaceful means to resolve a potentially violent situation, then and only then may you use Arnis de Mano as a legitimate means of self-defense. Even when defending yourself, you must ultimately decide which techniques to use, be it to evade, subdue or otherwise.

  • Should any visitor(s) enter the training hall, notify your instructor. If necessary, suspend the training session. Upon the instructor’s command, everyone must face the new arrivals then pugay.
  • Should you arrive late at a training session, wait at the back of the training hall\area of the class.
  • Should the need arise to be excused from formation or the training area, inform the instructor. When acknowledged, AVOID obstructing the instructors’ view of the training area or students by:

    Walking towards the instructor then behind him.
    Walking behind your row.
    Walking off BEHIND or to the SIDE of the training area.

The Arnisador must not measure efforts to improve or compare one’s self against those less favored in order to justify one’s own failings. They must not only understand knowledge, but must also protect all knowledge.

Learning Arnis de Mano requires 1,000 days. However, to understand the essence, the “spirit” of the Art, will take more than 10,000 days.

Fire is needed to boil a kettle of water. The water boils if the intensity is kept constant. Arnisador need to always keep the intensity of the inner fire

To achieve the purpose, comprehension and the enlightenment from “The way of the flow”. To achieve the way of the flow is to constantly acquire new knowledge and to practice it into a flow so that it blends in with your natural moves. “The flow is like a water fall that balances with the universe and maintain it as a wonder of nature, to seek the water’s source... always remain a mystery.”
