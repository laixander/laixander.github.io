Rules and Policies

DEKITI TIRSIA SIRADAS TORTAL INT’L. FIGHTING SYSTEM

RULES AND GUIDELINES OF AN INSTRUCTOR

  • Instructor must be certified by the organization.
  • Must have one certified instructor and one assistant instructor.
  • Instructor must have completed the instructor programs by the organization.
  • Students ranking must be authenticated by the organization.
  • Any school must have one seminar with instructor from headquarter once every two year.
  • All Schools and affiliate schools must ensure that the training programs instructed by the License Instructors are all standard being taught by the headquarter.

INSTRUCTOR RULES

The authorize Martial Art Instructor share a responsibility for the academic, emotional and physical development of trainees. Not only must instructor be completely knowledgeable regarding pertinent goals, objectives and subject matter, they must also zealously protect the health and safety of each trainee who is undergoing instruction.

Instructors serve as role models, teaching trainees how authority should be exercised. The pattern of behavior exhibited by instructors may be often be emulated by trainees when they serve their committees. Therefore, instructors must maintain a professional manner at all times.

TRAINEE SAFETY

The primary responsibility of an instructor is to ensure the safety of trainees who are under their immediate control and supervision. An instructor must be constantly alert to evidence that a trainee is experiencing a medical, physical or emotional problem that affects performance and take immediate corrective action. An instructor should be attentive to the following conditions:

Environment
A trainee shall not be exposed unnecessarily elements (cold, heat, etc.)

Amenities
A trainee shall not be denied rest, water or food as punitive measure.

Physical Exertion
A trainee shall not be required to continue in physical activities that are beyond the individual’s capability and capacity.

Training Area
A trainee shall not be required to engage in training activities on any surface that is likely to cause injury or unnecessary discomfort.

PHYSICAL CONTACT

It is the responsibility of an instructor to see that a trainee is not maltreated or physically abused. An instructor must be ever mindful of the following:

Physical Abuse
A trainee shall never be intentionally struck, shoved, dragged or otherwise subjected to physical abuse. Push-ups, sit-ups and other exercises are designed to enhance physical fitness. However, within the bounds of good judgment, these exercises may be used on an individual basis for motivation and alertness purposes.

Physical contact
Physical contact with trainees shall be limited to situations where it is essential to demonstrate a required training skill or procedure to prevent the occurrence of an injury or to render first aid.

Trainee’s Sex
Contact with trainee of the opposite sex shall be severely limited. When Contact is necessary or required it should not cause embarrassment to the trainee.

VERBAL COMMUNICATION

It is the responsibility of the instructor to communicate knowledge and information clearly, effectively and in a positive manner. An instructor should be aware of the following:

Abusive Language
A trainee shall not be the object of foul, abusive, profane or insulting language, except in role-playing situations when such language is used as a training device.

Disparaging Comments
A trainee shall not be the object or disparaging comments whether humorous or not, with respect to the individual’s race, religion, gender, ethnic background or agency.

TRAINEE DISCIPLINE

It is the responsibility of instructors to maintain discipline over trainees under their immediate control and supervision, to instill the principles of self-discipline in trainees and to avoid actions that adversely affect discipline. The following actions are unacceptable:

Favoritism
Evidencing favoritism to a trainee or a group of trainees.

Fraternizing
Socializing with a trainee or group of trainees either at the training school or off-site.

Gestures
The use of threatening, menacing or profane gestures.

Treatment
Failing to treat a trainee or a group of trainees in a fair and impartial manner.

Rules
Failure to enforce association rules and regulations in an equitable manner.

Misconduct
If any instructor shall reported for any illegal or unethical behavior or misconduct by his/ her trainees or other fellows.
